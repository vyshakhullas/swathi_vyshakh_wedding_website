Ceremony

The Wedding

A traditional Kerala Hindu wedding, blending customs passed down through generations. The day begins with Mangala Snanam, a ceremonial cleansing bath, ahead of the Muhurtham — the astrologically fixed auspicious hour. Family gathers around the Nilavilakku (ceremonial lamp) as garlands are exchanged and the Thali is tied, followed by Kanyadaanam and Saptapadi — seven steps taken together to seal our vows — to the sound of traditional Nadaswaram music.

Visiting Kerala

Travel Guide

Everything you need to plan your trip to Kozhikode and Thalassery for the celebrations.

Getting There

Calicut International Airport (CCJ) is closest to the wedding — about 25–30 minutes from Pantheerankaav and roughly 1.5 hours from Thalassery.

Kannur International Airport (CNN) is the easier option if you're mainly coming for the reception — about 45 minutes to an hour from Thalassery, and roughly 2 hours from the wedding venue.

Flying into Bangalore (Kempegowda International Airport, BLR)? It often has more international connections. From there, overnight buses run to Thalassery/Kannur (roughly 8–9 hours) — a comfortable, budget-friendly option if a direct flight isn't convenient.

By train, Kozhikode Railway Station and Thalassery Railway Station are both well connected to major cities across South India.

Where to Stay

  • Near Malhar Bhoomi / Pantheerankaav: hotels in central Kozhikode city, a short drive away.
  • Near the reception: Thalassery and nearby Kannur town have several comfortable hotel options.
  • We recommend booking early — December is peak wedding and tourist season in Kerala.

Things to Do

  • Walk along Kozhikode Beach at sunset, or explore the old town's markets and mosques.
  • Try the local food — Kozhikode is famous for its biryani, halwa, and seafood.
  • Thalassery is known for its own biryani style, historic fort, and circus heritage.
  • If you have extra days, consider a trip up to Wayanad's hills and wildlife (about 2–2.5 hours away).

Weather & What to Pack

Expect warm, mostly dry weather in December, with temperatures around 24–32°C (75–90°F).

Light, breathable clothing is best. Bring something modest and comfortable for the ceremony, and don't forget sunscreen and comfortable shoes for walking.

Local Etiquette

  • Modest clothing (shoulders and knees covered) is appreciated at both the ceremony and any temple visits.
  • You may be asked to remove footwear before entering the ceremony venue or homes.
  • Kerala is generally very welcoming and easygoing — don't hesitate to ask locals or hotel staff for help.
